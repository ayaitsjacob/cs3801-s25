What is Ethical Licensing & Open Source and Why is it Important?

Open Source is software whose source code is available to anyone to view and modify. Ethical licensing adds constraints to the way open source software will be used. While open source licenses allow freedom of use, ethical licenses help set boundaries to prevent misuse of software.

Having open access to software assists in reducing digital inequality, especially in areas with limited resources. It provides areas like schools and nonprofits with free software instead of purchasing licenses. Open source also promotes transparency by allowing anyone the chance to inspect how software works. Cautioning developers to take accountability for their work, as anyone can access it.

Open source software has fueled some of the worldâ€™s most important technological breakthroughs. However, traditional open source licenses often come with no restrictions, allowing anyone, including those developing surveillance tools, weapons, or technologies that violate human rights, to use the code. In response, a growing ethical licensing movement urges developers to add social responsibility clauses to their licenses. This ensures that their innovations are used to advance positive causes while helping prevent misuse and harm.

Applying Utilitarian Perspective

While open source software allows anyone to use code freely, influencing innovation, when there are no restrictions, the software can be used for harmful purposes. Hence, why we apply ethical licenses to prevent harm without outweighing the freedom and utility of open software. Through the lens of Utilitarianism, ethical licensing can be seen as a way to maximize benefits and minimize harm. By setting clear limits on harmful uses, developers help protect vulnerable communities and guide technology toward outcomes that benefit society as a whole. This approach ensures that the well-being and happiness of the broader public take precedence over the profits or freedoms of those who might misuse technology for harmful purposes.

How exactly did this become an issue?

In 2019, a group of developers founded the Ethical Source movement after realizing that their open-source technology was being used in government surveillance activities. In response, they created the Hippocratic License, which allows open use of software except when it is used to violate human rights. This endeavor triggered a broader discussion on how the IT community should strike a balance between the goals of transparency and ethical responsibility.
