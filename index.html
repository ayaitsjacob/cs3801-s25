Whistleblowing & Ethical Advocacy in Tech

Overview

Whistleblowing & Ethical Advocacy in Tech go hand in hand. Ethical Advocacy in Tech is advocacy for ethical practices of hiring in tech, designing with privacy, accessibility, and inclusive in mind, and it can even mean advocacy for sustainability and oversight for environmental impact. Whistleblowing is the act of a person, not seeing or feeling a tech workplace is practicing ethical advocacy so they take it upon themself to (in a way) force ethical advocacy by revealing information about activity within their tech workspace that they deemed illegal, immoral, illicit, unsafe, unethical or fraudulent.

Chelsea Manning

"I want people to see the truth... regardless of who they are... because without information, you cannot make informed decisions as a public."

Ethical Frameworks

KANTIAN ETHICS

Kantian Ethics is the moral philosophy that emphasizes duty, rules, and the intrinsic worth of individuals and morality is based on adherence to universal principles and duty. In the context of whistleblowing and ethical advocacy in tech, Kantian ethics emphasizes moral duty, honesty, and acting from principle. So, a whistleblower who exposes wrongdoing because it's the right thing to do is in the right because it is ethically required for them to do so even if the personal consequences are severe.

UTILITARIANISM

Utilitarianism is the idea that actions are right if they promote happiness and wrong if they promote the reverse, so it focuses on the consequences of one's actions. If a whistleblower prevents major harm or benefits society by whistleblowing to stop a tech company that is promoting the reverse, then it is morally justice.

Examples

CHRISTOPHER WYLIE

An example of whistleblowing in the pursuit of ethical advocacy was when Christopher Wylie exposed how millions of Facebook users' data were harvested without consent and used for political advertising and manipulation (notably in the Brexit vote and 2016 U.S. election). He did this without regard for his own wellbeing. Wylie became a whistleblower because they believed in the dangers of data misuse and manipulation in tech and under Kantian ethics, they had a moral duty to expose Facebook. As a result of Wylie's advocacy there was a massive backlash against Facebook which let to congressional hearings, and "General Data Protection Regulation" enforcement in Europe.

SUSAN FOLWER

A second explain of a whistleblower advocating for ethical practices in tech is when Susan Fowler published a blog posting detailed systemic sexual harassment and toxic culture at Uber, which management ignored. Fowler's reporting on Uber sparked the #METOO movement in tech and led to broader discussions about gender discrimination, the CEO of Uber Travis Kalanick resigned, and Uber overhauled its internal culture.
